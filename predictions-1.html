Plot temperature over time¶

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.plot(data['time'], data['temperature_2m (°C)'], label='Temperature (°C)')
plt.title('Temperature Over Time')
plt.xlabel('Time')
plt.ylabel('Temperature (°C)')
plt.legend()
plt.show()
  • The graph shows a clear seasonal temperature cycle from 2020 to 2025, with peaks in warmer months and dips in colder months. The pattern remains consistent, indicating stable annual fluctuations with occasional extreme variations

Correlation heatmap¶

In [7]:
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
sns.heatmap(data.corr(), annot=True, cmap='coolwarm')
plt.title('Correlation Heatmap')
plt.show()

The correlation heatmap shows relationships between different weather variables. Key observations:

  • Precipitation and Rain are highly correlated (0.99), indicating they often occur together.
  • Temperature and Humidity have a moderate negative correlation (-0.54), meaning higher temperatures are associated with lower humidity.
  • Pressure, Wind Speed, and Precipitation show weak correlations with other variables, suggesting minimal direct influence.
